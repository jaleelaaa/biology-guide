Topic 1: Cell Discovery and Cell Theory

1 Mark Questions

Q1. Who discovered the cell? 1 Mark

Answer: Robert Hooke (1665)

Q2. Name the scientist who coined the term 'protoplasm'. 1 Mark

Answer: Purkinje (1839)

Q3. What did Leeuwenhoek discover? 1 Mark

Answer: Free-living cells in pond water (bacteria and protozoa)

Q4. Who proposed the cell theory? 1 Mark

Answer: Schleiden (1838) and Schwann (1839)

2 Mark Questions

Q5. State the cell theory. 2 Marks

Answer:
  1. All living organisms are composed of cells and cell products
  2. All cells arise from pre-existing cells (Omnis cellula-e cellula - Virchow, 1855)

Q6. Mention two limitations of cell theory. 2 Marks

Answer:
  1. Viruses do not have a cellular structure
  2. RBCs in mammals lack nucleus and organelles

3 Mark Questions

Q7. Distinguish between prokaryotic and eukaryotic cells (any three points). 3 Marks

Feature Prokaryotic Cell Eukaryotic Cell
Nucleus No true nucleus, nucleoid region Well-defined nucleus with nuclear envelope
Size 1-10 Œºm 10-100 Œºm
Organelles No membrane-bound organelles Membrane-bound organelles present

4 Mark Questions

Q8. Explain the Fluid Mosaic Model of plasma membrane. 4 Marks

Proposed by: Singer and Nicolson (1972)

Structure:

  • Lipid Bilayer: Composed of phospholipids arranged in two layers
  • Quasi-fluid Nature: Lipids can move laterally, giving fluidity to membrane
  • Mosaic Pattern: Proteins are embedded in various patterns like a mosaic

Protein Types:

  • Integral Proteins: Embedded in lipid bilayer, can be partially or completely buried
  • Peripheral Proteins: Loosely attached to surface, lie on inner or outer surface

Functions: Selective permeability, transport, cell recognition

Topic 2: Cell Organelles - Structure and Function

1 Mark Questions

Q9. Name the powerhouse of the cell. 1 Mark

Answer: Mitochondria

Q10. Which organelle is called the 'suicidal bag' of the cell? 1 Mark

Answer: Lysosomes

Q11. What is the function of ribosomes? 1 Mark

Answer: Protein synthesis

Q12. Name the cell organelle responsible for photosynthesis. 1 Mark

Answer: Chloroplast

2 Mark Questions

Q13. What are cristae? Mention their significance. 2 Marks

Cristae are shelf-like infoldings of the inner mitochondrial membrane.

Significance: They increase the surface area for ATP synthesis during cellular respiration.

Q14. Distinguish between RER and SER. 2 Marks

RER SER
Has ribosomes attached No ribosomes
Protein synthesis and transport Lipid synthesis, detoxification

4 Mark Questions

Q15. Describe the structure and functions of the nucleus. 4 Marks

Structure:

  • Nuclear Envelope: Double membrane with nuclear pores
  • Nucleoplasm: Nuclear matrix containing chromatin
  • Chromatin: DNA + Histone proteins, condenses to form chromosomes during division
  • Nucleolus: Ribosomal RNA synthesis, no membrane

Functions:

  1. Genetic Control: Contains DNA, controls all cellular activities
  2. Heredity: Stores and transmits genetic information
  3. Ribosome Production: Nucleolus synthesizes rRNA
  4. Gene Expression: Regulates which genes are active

  • (c) Golgi apparatus - Protein synthesis

  • ‚úó (a) Nucleolus synthesizes rRNA (ribosomal RNA), NOT proteins

Q8. [NEET 2020] The quasi-fluid nature of lipid bilayer depends on:

  • (a) Its length
  • (b) Its affinity to water
  • (c) Types and nature of fatty acids ‚úì CORRECT ANSWER
  • (d) Presence of proteins
Detailed Explanation:
  • ‚úì Membrane fluidity depends on:
    • Saturated vs Unsaturated fatty acids: Unsaturated (with C=C double bonds) increase fluidity
    • Fatty acid chain length: Shorter chains = more fluid
    • Cholesterol content: Maintains fluidity
  • ‚úó (a) Length of membrane doesn't determine fluidity of lipid bilayer
  • ‚úó (b) Amphipathic nature affects arrangement, not the quasi-fluid property
  • ‚úó (d) Proteins float in the bilayer but don't determine its fluidity

Pattern: Reasoning-based, biochemistry | Difficulty: Hard

NEET 2019 Questions

Q9. [NEET 2019] In bacteria, the site of respiration is:

  • (a) Episome
  • (b) Plasmid
  • (c) Mesosome ‚úì CORRECT ANSWER
  • (d) Ribosome
Detailed Explanation:
  • ‚úì Mesosomes are infoldings of plasma membrane in bacteria
  • ‚úì Increase surface area for respiratory enzymes (similar to mitochondrial cristae)
  • ‚úì Also involved in: DNA replication, cell wall formation, cell division
  • ‚úó (a) Episome: Extrachromosomal DNA (can integrate into bacterial chromosome)
  • ‚úó (b) Plasmid: Circular extrachromosomal DNA (carries antibiotic resistance)
  • ‚úó (d) Ribosome: Protein synthesis (70S type in bacteria)

Q10. [NEET 2019] Which of the following is true for both mitochondria and chloroplasts?

  • (a) Both have their own DNA
  • (b) Both have 70S ribosomes
  • (c) Both are double membrane-bound
  • (d) All of the above ‚úì CORRECT ANSWER
Detailed Explanation:
  • ‚úì Common features (semi-autonomous organelles):

Q11. [NEET 2018] Which of the following statements is true for secretory cells?

  • (b) Rough Endoplasmic Reticulum (RER) is easily observed ‚úì CORRECT ANSWER
  • (c) Only Smooth Endoplasmic Reticulum (SER) is present
  • (d) Secretory granules are formed in the nucleus
Detailed Explanation:
  • ‚úì Secretory cells (pancreatic cells, plasma cells) produce and secrete proteins
  • ‚úì Abundant RER: Ribosomes synthesize secretory proteins
  • ‚úì Pathway: RER ‚Üí Golgi ‚Üí Secretory vesicles ‚Üí Exocytosis
  • ‚úó (a) Golgi is ESSENTIAL for packaging secretory proteins (can't be absent)

Q12. [NEET 2018] Ribosomes are produced in:

  • (a) Nucleolus ‚úì CORRECT ANSWER
  • (b) Cytoplasm
  • (c) Golgi body
  • (d) Mitochondria
Detailed Explanation:
  • ‚úì Nucleolus = Site of rRNA synthesis and ribosome assembly
  • ‚úì Process:
    1. rRNA genes transcribed in nucleolus
    2. Ribosomal proteins (made in cytoplasm) imported
    3. rRNA + proteins assemble into ribosomal subunits
    4. Subunits exported to cytoplasm
  • ‚úó (b) Cytoplasm: Where ribosomes FUNCTION, not where they're produced
  • ‚úó (c) Golgi: Modifies and packages proteins
  • ‚úó (d) Mitochondria: Produce ATP (have own 70S ribosomes but don't make cell's 80S ribosomes)

Pattern: Factual, nucleus function | Difficulty: Easy

Q18. [NEET 2014] A cell lacking cell wall, plastids, and having centrioles is:

  • (a) Algal cell
  • (b) Fungal cell
  • (c) Plant cell
  • (d) Animal cell ‚úì CORRECT ANSWER
Detailed Explanation:
  • ‚úì Animal cell characteristics:
    • NO cell wall (only plasma membrane)

  • ‚úó (a) Algae: Have cell wall + chloroplasts

  • ‚úó (c) Plants: Have cell wall + plastids, NO centrioles

Pattern: Analytical, elimination-based | Difficulty: Easy
